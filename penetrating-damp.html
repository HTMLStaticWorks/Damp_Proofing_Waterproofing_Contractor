Penetrating Damp Repair & Coatings

How porous bricks, roofing leaks, and structural joint cracks cause moisture penetration.

Causes of Penetrating Damp

Penetrating damp occurs when moisture enters the building through external brickwork walls, windows, or roof structural systems. Unlike rising damp, it can happen at any level of the building.

The primary cause is damaged exterior brickwork. Over time, lime mortar joints wash away, bricks become highly porous, or structural cracks form. In heavy rain, these porous surfaces act like sponges, absorbing water and transferring it into internal plaster walls.

Primary Sources of Ingress

  • Porous bricks that lose their weather resistance.
  • Defective pointing in mortar structural joints.
  • Cracked external stucco render coats.
  • Defective downpipes and blocked roof gutters.
Solutions

How We Solve Penetrating Damp

Our solutions seal external brickwork masonry while maintaining breathing characteristics.

Silicone Waterproof Creams

We spray hydrophobic cream coatings onto external brickwork. The cream penetrates up to 12mm deep, creating a water-repelling barrier without altering masonry breathing profiles.

Structural Crack Stitching

Inserting high-strength stainless steel helical reinforcement bars into cracked masonry joints to restore load capacity and seal gaps.

Lime Mortar Repointing

Raking out failed joint sands and replacing them with premium breathable lime mortar mixes to defend mortar structures from frost damage.
