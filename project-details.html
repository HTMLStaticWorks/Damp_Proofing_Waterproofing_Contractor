Case Study #4810

Victorian Crypt Tanking & Drainage

Engineering-grade basement waterproofing to secure a flooded historical vault.

Before Treatment
After Protection

Project Brief & Diagnostic Assessment

The property suffered from severe groundwater flooding due to failure of an old cement rendering coating. Free water was entering through the floor-to-wall joints. Pinless moisture meters recorded critical levels (>95% WME) across the lower brickwork arches.

Treatments Applied

We chose a dual-protection Type C Cavity Drainage System, combined with localized Type A waterproof barriers to restrict soil moisture. Timbers in floor support joists were treated to prevent dry rot infection.

Cavity Membrane Installation

Applying high-density studded sheets to walls and floors, creating an air space to allow water to drain away without contacting plaster surfaces.

Perimeter Drainage Channels

Cutting channels into the concrete floor slab and installing drainage pipes to lead water safely to the collection sump.

Technical Details

  • Location: Manchester Heritage Vault
  • Moisture Drop: 98% (95% down to 14%)
  • Materials: 8mm Cavity membrane, channels, sump pump
  • BS Compliance: BS 8102:2022 (Type C)
